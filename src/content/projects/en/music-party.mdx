This project takes the Launchpad as its starting point, which embodies one instrument in each block and combine them into music. Such intuitive idea of triggering music just by moving your body. I wanted to build something that requires no musical training and no setup: you can pull a friend in at any moment and create an improvised beat together through movement and position, or set it up on a street corner and let strangers unknowingly spark a burst of music as they walk by — a fleeting, accidental act of collective creation. Music doesn't have to belong to a stage. It can happen anywhere, between anyone.  

The project is built with p5.js for the interactive canvas, using ml5.js's bodyPose model to detect human keypoints from a live webcam feed in real time. The screen is divided into a grid of cells, each mapped to a looping audio track; when any part of the body enters a cell, its corresponding track is activated. At the same time, the distance between the user's eyes is calculated to estimate their proximity to the camera — this distance is then mapped onto an A natural minor scale, with Tone.js synthesizing melodic notes in real time. The closer you are, the lower the note; the further away, the higher it climbs.
