This project draws inspiration from Jacob Collier's freestyle audience interactions, visual novel games, and the meta-narrative structure of The Stanley Parable. The goal was to create a live performance where the audience doesn't just watch — their choices actively shape the story's direction. Through the process of developing the script, the narrator gradually became an AI character, with the audience recast as its training data and me, the performer, as the user prompting it. This framing gave the whole performance a quiet underlying question: what happens when an AI begins to realize it is one?  

On the technical side, the project is built around p5.js, using p5Speech to voice the AI narrator and Hydra to generate visuals that shift in real time with the narrative. To prevent speech queue overflow from crashing the browser, I rewrote the speech logic to load one segment at a time, only queuing the next line after the current one finishes. All music was composed in advance, preloaded at zero volume, and controlled through a single volume parameter function that syncs each track to the story's progression. The visual layer was unified by feeding the p5.js canvas directly into Hydra as a source, keeping the entire performance interface integrated within one output.
